## What It Is

![Orazan biological monitoring system](/photos/orazan/photo-1.webp)

Biological toxicity monitoring observes changes in living organisms exposed to a water sample. The current Orazan product record describes live fish, camera-based behavioural analysis, and an AI-based alerting method. Measured behaviours include movement speed, swimming height, path characteristics, spacing and grouping, growth, and fish count.

This type of system can indicate that conditions have changed, but it does not by itself identify a contaminant, quantify its concentration, prove water safety, or guarantee detection of every harmful event. It should complement validated chemical, microbiological, process, and operational controls.

## Why It Matters

- **Broad biological response:** A living-organism response may provide a different signal from a single-parameter chemical probe.
- **Continuous observation:** Automated monitoring can support earlier review between laboratory samples when sensor health and alarm quality are controlled.
- **Actionable evidence:** An alarm needs confirmatory sampling, escalation criteria, trained ownership, and a tested response plan.
- **Ethical and operational care:** Species selection, acclimatisation, water conditions, welfare, mortality, cleaning, calibration, maintenance, and replacement procedures affect both performance and responsible operation.

## Documented Orazan Capabilities

The product record lists a typical population of 8 to 10 fish, Tiger Barb as a recommended species, local and network alarms, SMS notification, a Linux-based application, and a maintenance interval greater than 30 days. These are product-record statements that still require confirmation for the delivered version and operating environment.

The current evidence does not support the previously published chemical-sensor table, pH, turbidity, conductivity, chlorine or dissolved-oxygen ranges, ISO 7027 method, one-minute sampling, under-five-minute detection, IP65 enclosure, solar or battery operation, 72-hour buffering, automated valve closure, Modbus, OPC-UA, or zero false negatives.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What does Orazan detect?**
A: It analyses changes in fish behaviour that may correlate with toxic stress. It is not a contaminant-specific analyser. Establish sensitivity, specificity, false-positive and false-negative rates for agreed substances, concentrations, water chemistry, temperature, species condition, and baseline method.

**Q: How quickly does it alarm?**
A: No universal validated detection time is published in the current evidence. Measure time to biological response, algorithm decision, notification, operator acknowledgement, confirmatory sample, and recovery for the target scenario.

**Q: Does it integrate with SCADA?**
A: Network alarm output is mentioned, but no specific industrial protocol or electrical interface is established. Confirm the data model, interface, protocol, timestamp, security, alarm acknowledgement, and supported SCADA or historian versions.

**Q: Can an alarm automatically close a distribution valve?**
A: Automatic process action is not documented and should not be inferred. Any automated response needs hazard analysis, independent confirmation where required, fail-safe logic, authority, manual override, testing, and regulatory approval.
