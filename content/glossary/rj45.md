## Definition

RJ45 is the standardised 8-position, 8-conductor (8P8C) modular connector used for Ethernet connections. It is the physical interface for 10/100/1000BASE-T (copper Gigabit Ethernet) and is ubiquitous in enterprise, industrial, and consumer networking.

## How It Works

An RJ45 connector mates with an RJ45 jack and provides eight contact positions carrying the differential transmit and receive pairs for Ethernet. IEEE 802.3ab (1000BASE-T) uses all four pairs for simultaneous bidirectional transmission at 1 Gbps over Cat5e or Cat6 cables.

## Why It Matters for Network Security

Copper Ethernet via RJ45 is used in many OT environments alongside fibre. For data diodes with copper interfaces, the one-way barrier is implemented in the electronics rather than in the optical physics — typically using TX-only and RX-only circuits with hardware-disabled return pins.
