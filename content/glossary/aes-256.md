## Definition

AES-256 is the 256-bit key variant of the Advanced Encryption Standard. It uses 14 rounds of the AES substitution-permutation network and is the variant required by most high-assurance security frameworks for the protection of sensitive and classified data.

## How It Works

A 256-bit key provides 2²⁵⁶ possible keys. The best known cryptanalytic attacks against AES-256 — including related-key attacks — require computational effort that remains infeasible with any foreseeable classical computing resources. In practice, AES-256 security reduces to the security of the key management system: a well-implemented AES-256 cipher is broken by stealing keys, not by breaking the algorithm.

## Why It Matters for Network Security

AES-256 is the minimum key length mandated by NSA Suite B for TOP SECRET data, by FIPS 140-3 Level 3+ implementations, and by most national-level security standards. For network encryption of sensitive OT or government traffic, AES-256 provides a substantial security margin beyond the 128-bit variant.
