## Definition

NIAP (National Information Assurance Partnership) is a US government program that oversees Common Criteria evaluations for IT products intended for national security applications. Products on the NIAP Approved Products List (APL) have been evaluated against Protection Profiles by NIAP-accredited laboratories.

## How It Works

Product vendors submit their products for evaluation against a specific Protection Profile (PP) — a standardised security specification for a product category (data diodes, firewalls, VPN gateways). An accredited laboratory conducts the evaluation, and NIAP reviews and approves the results. Approved products appear on the APL.

## Why It Matters for Network Security

US government and defense procurement for national security systems requires products on the NIAP APL. International partners and export customers in NATO countries also reference NIAP APL status as a quality indicator.
