## Definition

IEC 62443 is an international series of standards for the security of Industrial Automation and Control Systems (IACS). It defines a framework of security levels (SL-0 through SL-4), zones and conduits, and requirements for system integrators, product suppliers, and asset owners.

## How It Works

IEC 62443 organises IACS security into four zones defined by their security requirements and interconnected by conduits. Conduits must implement security controls appropriate to the higher of the two zones they connect. Data diodes are recognised as a hardware implementation of a conduit control at the highest security levels (SL-3 and SL-4), where software-only controls are insufficient.

## Why It Matters for Network Security

IEC 62443 is the primary international reference for OT security and is referenced by national regulations in energy, water, and manufacturing sectors worldwide. Compliance documentation referencing IEC 62443 zones and conduits is frequently required for critical-infrastructure operators.
