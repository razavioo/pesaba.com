## Definition

SDH (Synchronous Digital Hierarchy) is a standard for data transmission over optical fibre, defined by ITU-T G.707. It provides a synchronous frame structure for multiplexing lower-rate tributaries into higher-rate optical signals, with built-in operations, administration, and maintenance (OAM) functions.

## How It Works

SDH frames are synchronous: all network elements derive their timing from a common reference clock. The basic SDH rate is STM-1 at 155.52 Mbps; higher rates are STM-4 (622 Mbps), STM-16 (2.5 Gbps), and STM-64 (10 Gbps). SDH multiplex sections carry tributary containers (C-12 for 2 Mbps E1, C-4 for 140 Mbps) organised into virtual containers (VC-12, VC-4).

## Why It Matters for Network Security

SDH networks carry critical government, utility, and telecom traffic across Iran and the wider region. Many existing transmission links use SDH infrastructure. Pesaba products allow Ethernet and IP traffic to be carried over these legacy SDH links, extending the network lifetime of existing infrastructure.
