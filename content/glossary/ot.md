## Definition

OT (Operational Technology) refers to hardware and software that monitors and controls physical devices, processes, and infrastructure in industrial and critical-infrastructure environments — power grids, oil and gas, manufacturing, water treatment, transportation.

## How It Works

OT environments consist of PLCs, RTUs, DCS controllers, SCADA systems, HMIs, and field devices. They run deterministic, real-time processes where timing and reliability are more critical than confidentiality. OT systems were traditionally isolated from IP networks ("air-gapped") but have become increasingly connected to corporate IT systems.

## Why It Matters for Network Security

OT systems control physical processes with potentially life-safety consequences. Unlike IT breaches — which primarily affect data — OT breaches can cause equipment damage, environmental incidents, or loss of life. The convergence of OT and IT networks has dramatically expanded the attack surface.
