## Definition

SFP (Small Form-factor Pluggable) is a compact, hot-pluggable optical transceiver module used to connect network equipment (switches, routers, media converters) to fibre-optic or copper cabling. SFP supports speeds up to 1 Gbps; SFP+ supports 10 Gbps; QSFP+ supports 40 Gbps; QSFP28 supports 100 Gbps.

## How It Works

An SFP module contains a laser transmitter (Tx) and photodetector receiver (Rx) packaged in a standardised housing that plugs into an SFP cage on the host device. Single-mode SFP modules use 1310 nm or 1550 nm lasers and can reach distances from 10 km to 80 km. Multi-mode modules use 850 nm VCSELs and cover 300 m to 550 m.

## Why It Matters for Network Security

In data diode implementations, SFP modules are used asymmetrically: only a transmitter SFP is installed on the source side and only a receiver SFP on the destination side, creating the physical one-way barrier. No electrical signal can traverse the glass in the reverse direction.
