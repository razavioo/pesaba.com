## Definition

Walk testing is the indoor equivalent of drive testing — measuring mobile network coverage, signal quality, and data performance by walking through buildings, tunnels, malls, and other indoor environments with portable measurement equipment.

## How It Works

Walk-test equipment is handheld or carried in a backpack. It includes measurement terminals, a compact GPS or indoor positioning system, and logging software. Indoor environments present unique challenges: signal penetration from outdoor base stations varies with building materials, and distributed antenna systems (DAS) or small cells are common.

## Why It Matters for Network Security

A growing proportion of mobile traffic originates indoors. Poor indoor coverage is a primary driver of subscriber dissatisfaction and churn. Walk tests provide the ground-truth data needed to optimise DAS configurations, identify coverage holes, and validate new indoor deployments.
