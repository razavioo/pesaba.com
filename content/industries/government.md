## The Challenge

Government networks handle classified communications, citizen data, inter-ministry coordination, and connections to critical national infrastructure. The threat environment is sophisticated — nation-state adversaries, insider threats, and supply-chain attacks are all in scope. Procurement requirements emphasise sanctions-resilient sourcing, local content, and certifiable security properties.

## Why It Matters

- **Classification boundary enforcement:** Government networks operate across multiple classification levels. Data crossing from a higher classification zone to a lower one must be controlled by hardware that provides a verifiable, one-way guarantee — not a software firewall that can be configured incorrectly.
- **Sovereignty requirements:** Hardware deployed in sensitive government environments must have a verifiable supply chain, local firmware, and no dependency on foreign cloud services for operation.
- **Continuous operation:** Government networks must operate through cyber incidents. Hardware isolation devices that fail-closed — dropping traffic rather than forwarding insecure traffic — maintain security posture even under attack.

## How Pesaba Solves It

Pesaba data diodes enforce classification boundaries using FPGA hardware with no operating system and no software attack surface. The devices are manufactured in Iran, use a documented and locally verified supply chain, and operate autonomously without cloud connectivity. For inter-network encrypted links between government facilities, EMX-6 provides AES-256-GCM at line rate on standard Ethernet.
